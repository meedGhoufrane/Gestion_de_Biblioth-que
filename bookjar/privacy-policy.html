Spider-Themes Privacy Policy & Data Collection (GDPR) Disclosure

Last Revision Date: 31 October 2024

At Spider-Themes, we respect and recognize our users’ right to privacy and their right to know how and why we collect, use, protect, and disclose their personal information.

 A. Accountability 

Spider-Themes (“Spider-Themes”, “we”, “us”, “our”) provides industry-leading themes & plugins to help web developers and website owners/operators quickly and easily build professional WordPress websites. Our clients are mostly experienced website creators or operators who are familiar with the hows and whys of privacy rules.

We collect information about the content of the privacy policy and the operation of cookies and related technologies. However, users do not require any special knowledge or experience to understand this policy.

This Privacy Policy outlines how we collect, use, disclose, protect, and dispose of personal information, as well as the rights that customers have regarding their information.

 B. Personal Information 

Two types of personal information are protected by privacy rights.

The first is information that identifies a specific person (alone or in combination with other information).

A person’s name is personal information if it is unique. An individual’s name combined with a second piece of information is an example of personal information if it is not unique (and most people’s names are not). Home Address Email Address Phone Number Social Media Identifier Age Nickname Detailed description of physical characteristics The neighborhood the person lives in Where they went to school and their medical history are examples of personally identifiable information that when combined with a name or other information can constitute a Personal Information identifier.

Another type of protected personal information is information of any personal nature about an identifiable individual.

Examples of such personal information include:

  • The person’s race, national or ethnic origin, color, religion, age, sex, sexual orientation, or marital or family status.
  • The person’s education or medical psychiatry psychological criminal or employment history.
  • The person’s financial situation or information relating to financial transactions in which the person is involved.
  • The person’s social security number or other unique number or identifier assigned to them.
  • The person’s fingerprints or blood type.
  • An individual’s personal opinion or perspective on a social or political issue.
  • The perception or opinion of others about the person.
  • In general, any information that could prove embarrassing or damaging to an individual’s reputation, or that no reasonable person would want to make public.

Due to the nature of our business and the reasons why customers visit our website and transact with us, the personal information we collect and use is limited to:

  • Information needed to communicate with customers: Name Email Address Phone Number WhatsApp Skype or similar ID and/or Social Media Messaging Service.
  • Information that helps us improve our products, services, websites, and business processes.
  • Information for conducting customer relationship audits management programs and marketing activities, including identifying product improvements and additional products or services that may be of interest to customers.
  • Information that helps us better manage and improve our customers’ overall relationship with us, so they can continue to do business with us, including improving the user experience on our website.
  • Regarding points four to six in this list, please refer specifically to the information in the “Other Information We Collect” section of this policy

  • Notify customers of the breach if their personal information has been or may have been accessed or compromised.
  • Inform customers if know that specific personal information has been or may have been accessed or affected.
  • Inform customers of steps they can take to reduce the risk of harm from a breach, such as changing passwords or monitoring accounts.
  • Notify customers when the breach has been resolved and data risk has been reduced.
  • If required by law, notify the applicable government authority of the violation.
